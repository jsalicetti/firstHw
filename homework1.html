About Me

Hello everyone, name is Jessie. I am 27 years young, born and raised in the Bronx. I've always known I was a creative person who enjoyed getting their hands dirty, as a curious kid who'd break apart old electronics to attempt to put it back together or as a young trouble maker who'd steal my mothers tool box to see what each tool did on various things in the household. As I reached my twenties I began to see this as a real problem I had to fix. In my mind this weakness I had was a constant state of boredom in which doing the same mundane activities day in and day out was just not something I could invision myself doing for the next fourty years of my life. Growing up in the environment in which I did I didn't have any family members that went to college or any role models in the corporate world. The only successful people I knew in this concrete jungle were athletes, musicians, models etc. all things you had to be obsessed over faithfully and continously. I hoped I could pick one of my many interests and stick with in for as long as I could, then I'd figure out exactly what it is I wanted to do. I then stumbled on a gov tech "influencer" on Instagram, and while I won't get into details about what the video was about I can say with certaintly I'd found what it was I could realistically stick to, that I would actually enjoy! Something I had been doing and using on a daily basis for years anyway. Jumping into the world of technology!

Fast forward to the Spring of 2023, I have now researched online the best path (according to the masters of the universe on Reddit) to getting into the Tech field without a degree. I knew financially it wasn't optimal for me to go back to school so I began my journey of self study. I read and watched anything I could get my hands on that fit the path of where I was trying to go. I began taking courses on coursera to study the basics before I decided to schedule any exams like the CompTIA A+ or CompTIA Security + exams. In this journey I learned that networking is key in this feild, so I reached out to my friend from high school who had informed me that he worked at a tech company in Manhattan. He invited me to the office to chit chat and network, while there I asked non stop questions for 2 hours and even ended up getting a parking ticket because I was not leaving that office until I felt that I really utilized my time there. A current cyber security consultant there told me to apply for an apprenticeship program at his company to see if I could get my foot in the door. That night I went home and immediately applied for the only apprenticeship this company had which had been posted almost 30 weeks prior to that date. I knew the probabilty of getting that apprenticeship was low however I never lost hope, I continued my studies, continued to look for people that worked there on linkedin in order to network. Almost a year later I found myself part of an apprenticeship opportunity through an amazing company called peopleshores, partnered with this company I had fought so hard to get my foot in the door. I finally did it, my foot was finally in the door, I finally got to Accenture.
